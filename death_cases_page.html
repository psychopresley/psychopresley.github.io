II. Death rate


Some broadcast medias try to illustrate the devastation caused by the coronavirus by showing the deaths/100k numbers. Despite being a valid metric, I don't like to use it in this case and the reason is quite simple: A person can't die from covid if he/she is not down with the covid. By applying this methodology they end up putting healthy and unhealthy people in the same baskett and this is a huge mistake that leads people to underestimate its mortality.

The graphic below shows the mortality rate of the covid-19 by each country as a deaths/confirmed ratio. The graphic is interactive so hover over the dots, select a specific label for zooming and explore the infos. The numbers of Iemen, with an ongoing civil war are devastating. More than a quarter of the infected dies. The top 5 is composed by the poorest countries of the eastern mediterranean and latin america

Moreover the death rate is the right parameter to check for health system collapse. There's no oficial base number adopted to indicate when the country's health system has collapsed but I would guess, based on my readings in the media that any country with death rate higher than 1.7% have at some point and at some extension faced some collapse in their health system.
